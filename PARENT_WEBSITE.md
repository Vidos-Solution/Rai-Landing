### 3. What Responsible AI Means in Practice

**Key Message:**  
Responsible AI is not about abstract ethics or technical theory.

**In professional environments, it means:**

- **Knowing when AI can — and cannot — be used**
  - Understanding appropriate use cases
  - Recognizing limitations and boundaries
  - Making informed decisions about AI adoption

- **Understanding how AI influences judgement and decisions**
  - Awareness of AI's impact on professional judgment
  - Recognizing cognitive biases in AI-assisted work
  - Maintaining professional autonomy and critical thinking

- **Managing risk, bias, and accountability**
  - Identifying and mitigating AI-related risks
  - Addressing bias in AI systems and outputs
  - Establishing clear accountability structures

- **Being able to explain and justify AI-assisted outputs**
  - Transparency in AI usage
  - Documentation and traceability
  - Ability to defend AI-assisted decisions

**In short:** Using AI in a way you can stand behind.

### 4. The Responsible AI Governance Framework (RAIGF)

**Introduction:**  
The RAIGF is the foundation of the Vidos RAI platform.

**What It Does:**  
It translates Responsible AI principles into:

- **Practical governance structures**
  - Operational frameworks, not theoretical concepts
  - Clear organizational roles and responsibilities
  - Scalable implementation models

- **Clear roles and decision points**
  - Defined governance roles (User, Reviewer, Steward, Sponsor)
  - Decision-making processes and approval workflows
  - Accountability chains

- **Defined controls and evidence**
  - Specific controls for AI use
  - Documentation requirements
  - Evidence collection and retention

- **Assurance and reporting that stands up to scrutiny**
  - Audit-ready structures
  - Compliance reporting capabilities
  - Professional standards alignment

**Framework Characteristics:**  
The framework is designed to be **operational, scalable, and auditable** — not theoretical.

### 5. Standards and Professional Alignment

**RICS Alignment:**  
The RAIGF is mapped against the **RICS Responsible AI principles (September 2025)**, supporting consistent and defensible AI use in professional services.

**Additional Standards Integration:**  
The framework is also informed by widely recognised Responsible AI themes reflected in:

- **ISO/IEC AI governance and risk standards**
  - International best practices
  - Risk management frameworks
  - Governance structures

- **EU AI regulatory principles**
  - EU AI Act compliance
  - European regulatory requirements
  - Cross-border considerations

- **UK AI guidance**
  - UK-specific regulatory expectations
  - Professional services standards
  - National guidance alignment

**Why This Matters:**  
This ensures the framework remains relevant as expectations and regulation evolve.
